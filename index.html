Sreevidya Ayyar

I am a third-year PhD student in Economics at the London School of Economics and Political Science. I am interested in questions at the intersection of Development Economics and Econometrics; specifically, I am interested in identification, estimation and inference in experimental settings with network effects.

I hold a B.A. (Hons) in Economics and Management from the University of Oxford, and an MSc in Econometrics and Mathematical Economics from the London School of Economics. I have previously worked as a research assistant on papers in human capital development and Bayesian meta analysis.

We discuss using Wasserstein Generative Adversarial Networks (WGANs) as a method for systematically generating artificial data that mimic closely any given real data set without the researcher having many degrees of freedom. We apply the methods to compare in three different settings twelve different estimators for average treatment effects under unconfoundedness.
